Context

With only a week to present a prototype for the Vehicle History Report, the Design Sprint methodology emerged as the ideal approach. This incredible opportunity not only allowed me to learn but also to expand my capabilities significantly.

The Challenge

How might we

offer relevant, accessible and fast information on used cars?

Problem statement

Argentines are not used to buying vehicle history reports.

Proposal

Design the landing page and the vehicle history report.

Design Process

Due to time constraints it was decided to work a full week to tackle the project. This first week would serve as a guide to move forward with the project.

The test step was discontinued for reasons unrelated to the project.

Understand

Context by numbers

The consultation on used cars increased by 17% during 2020.

Relevant points
  • Use of color to communicate the most important information to the user.
  • Apply icons to facilitate information.
  • Don't use too much text, instead of using short texts.
  • Shows relevant information at the top of the report.
  • Give more blank spaces to generate more relaxation for reading.

Sketch

We quickly sketch out some landing page ideas and discuss how to improve the reporting request.

Define

Who is our user?

We analyze two possible groups:

BTC: The final consumer. People who want to buy a used car.
BTB: The companies that can provide the report to the final consumer.

User journey

We found two moments when the report is crucial to buying a car. First, before checking the car and second after checking the car. In these two moments we can offer different prices to facilitate and motivate the purchase.

First launch

It was decided to start working only in the best-selling used car areas.
